Acknowledgements

 

This book was planned as a two year project, but in fact took ten years to complete.  A project of such length must take a huge toll of energy and commitment from the author.  During the long years of writing, I became conscious of how much I owed to the support of the people around me.

 

Amongst the academics, I single out Professor Graham Birtwistle, for his good nature and good advice, coming from a man whose history in computer science reaches back far further than my own.  His personal example, in a department that became increasingly impersonal, was a reminder of a different and better set of standards for treating people. I must also add Dr Willi Riha, who as a friend accompanied me in perambulations around Golden Acre Park in discussions on computer science.  I wish them good luck in their retirements.

 

Professor David Hogg was unfailingly supportive and it is thanks largely to him that Qi and Qi/Tk have appeared under the GNU licence.

 

Dr Antoni Diller and I have been correspondents since Antoni’s employment as a research assistant at Leeds on the THRILLS proof assistant.  His contribution to the architecture of THRILLS owed much to the profound humour of Lewis Carroll, who, I’m sure, would have appreciated some of the finer points of the design.  Antoni supported the production of this book and his sense of the absurd preserved me from taking things too seriously. Professor Maria Faé, both friend and colleague, made an essential contribution to the final chapter of this book through her knowledge of transport engineering.  Dr Magdalena Pamesa was a constant source of support in the final stages of this book.

 

I would also like to extend my thanks to Steffen Bakel of Imperial and Stefan Kahrs of Canterbury University for their valuable comments. Also to Delia Kesner of the University of Paris for her correspondence on types and patterns, and to Bernhard Beckert of the University of Koblenz for his correspondence on tableau theorem proving. Rod Burstall was very supportive during my time at the LFCS and Robin Milner provided useful clarification on the history of SML.  I’d also like to thank Werner Hett, for permission to draw from his list of programming challenges.  Carl Schapiro of SRI picked out many minor bugs in a preprint of this book as well as bugs in Qi/Tk. itself

 

Mark Tarver, 

January 2005.
